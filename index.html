🇬🇷 Greek Golden Visa

Everything about the Greek Golden Visa

Europe’s most accessible residency-by-investment programme — a 5-year permit for you and your family, in one of the Mediterranean’s strongest property markets.

Check your eligibility

5 facts

What the Greek Golden Visa gives you

A residence permit granted to non-EU nationals who make a qualifying investment in Greece — most commonly in real estate. Governed by the Hellenic Republic under Law 4251/2014, as amended.

01.

Demand keeps climbing

Issuances to investors and their families have grown from a few hundred a year to tens of thousands — Greece is now the last major EU programme offering residency through real estate.

02.

Lowest entry threshold in the EU 🇪🇺

A minimum investment of €250,000 for commercial-to-residential conversions or the restoration of listed buildings — the lowest qualifying threshold of any golden-visa programme in the European Union.

03.

No relocation, no minimum stay

There is no requirement to live in Greece to keep the permit. It is valid for 5 years and renewable for as long as the investment is held.

04.

You can rent the property

You can lease your property on a long-term basis, creating a stable, passive income stream while you keep the Golden Visa.

Good to know: under current Greek law, Golden-Visa real estate may be let on long-term leases, but short-term (Airbnb-style) rentals are not permitted on properties used to qualify.

05.

Family inclusion — a 3-generation policy

A single investment covers the main applicant, their spouse (or registered partner), children up to 21 (or up to 24 in some cases), and the parents of both spouses.

Investment requirement

How much you need to invest

Since 1 September 2024, real-estate thresholds depend on the region. Conversion and restoration projects keep the €250,000 route open across the country.
